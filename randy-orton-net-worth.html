Randy Orton Net Worth: Salary &amp; Lifestyle

An American professional wrestler and actor Randal Keith Orton, better known under his ring name Randy Orton, has a net worth of $7 million.

Orton is considered one of the greatest professional wrestlers of all time who is currently signed to WWE and performs on the RAW brand.

Being born into a wrestling household, all the wrestling techniques came to him as a hereditary gift.

Randy Orton is the third generation of wrestlers of his family, his father Bob Orton Jr., grandfather Bob Orton Sr., and Uncle Barry Orton were all professional wrestlers.

Moreover, Orton held the World Heavyweight Championship four times and WWE Championship 10 times.

Similarly, he became the final holder of the World Heavyweight Championship, which later became WWE World Heavyweight Champion at TLC: Tables, Ladders & Chairs in 2013.

Furthermore, he won Money in the Bank Ladder Match two times in 2013 and 2009. Similarly, he headlined several WWE pay-per-view events, including WrestleMania XXX and WrestleMania 25.

Randy Orton | Lifestyle and Vacations

Lifestyle

Randy Orton married Samantha Speno on 21st September 2007 and had a daughter on 12th July 2008 named Alanna.

Their marriage fell apart in 2012, and the couple legally separated in 2013.

WWE superstar had to pay a six-figure for their divorce settlement, $600,000 to be more precise, and Speno also got the full custody of their daughter.

Apart from divorce settlement money, she also got several pieces of jewelry and other comforts, costing Orton a huge fortune.

After two years, the Viper married Kimberly Kessler on 14th November 2015. Together they have a daughter named Brooklyn.

Moreover, over the years, Randy has been into several controversies. When Sports Illustrated published an article related to growth hormone use within the athletic community, Orton’s name was also brought up.

They claimed that he used performance enhancement drugs and growth hormones to enhance his physical ability.

Besides that, he was also investigated by the WWE for sexual harassment. However, there was no proof, and nothing came of the investigation or the allegations.

As for his daily lifestyle, Orton has a pretty strict workout regime, given his profession. He has to stay fit and healthy for his work.

Aside from that, the Viper has shared several pictures on his social media handle flexing luxurious timepieces from Swiss brand Rolex.

The exact model of the watch is not identified, but a Rolex costs anywhere from $5k to $10k or more. He owns at least three of these watches.

Randy Orton | Career

Randy Orton was born in Knoxville, Tennessee, the United States on 1st April 1980. Orton’s mother was a nurse, and his father was also a professional wrestler.

His parents tried their best to persuade him not to go into a wrestling career, but wrestling was in his blood.

He attended Hazelwood Central High School, and upon graduation in 1998, he joined the Marines. However, he was discharged for his bad conduct in 1999.

After getting discharged from the Marines all, that was left for him was wrestling. So he debuted in wrestling in 2001 at Mid-Missouri Wrestling Association-Southern Illinois Conference Wrestling.

After a year, Randy was signed by WWE (then WWF) and was sent to Kentucky to continue his training, and the rest is history.
